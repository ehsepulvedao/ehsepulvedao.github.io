I am an **Associate Professor (Maître de Conférences)** in Industrial Engineering and Maintenance at the **Université de Perpignan Via Domitia (UPVD)** and a researcher at **PROMES-CNRS (UPR 8521)** within the **CSPG (Control, Storage and Management of Electrical Grids)** research team.

My research focuses on the development of **artificial intelligence, fault diagnosis, predictive maintenance, prognostics, and decision-support systems** for renewable energy systems, smart grids, industrial processes, and cyber-physical systems. My objective is to develop robust, interpretable, and deployable AI solutions capable of improving the reliability, resilience, and sustainability of complex engineering systems.

I obtained my PhD from **Université Toulouse III – Paul Sabatier** and **LAAS-CNRS** in Toulouse, France, through a CIFRE industrial research program dedicated to fault diagnosis and performance loss assessment in large-scale photovoltaic power plants. This work led to the development of novel AI-based monitoring approaches for renewable energy applications and contributed to a joint industrial patent. My doctoral research was recognized with the **Prix de Thèse 2024** awarded by the École Doctorale GEETS.

Before joining UPVD, I worked as a **Postdoctoral Researcher at LAAS-CNRS**, where I developed artificial intelligence methods for battery energy storage systems, power electronics, and autonomous electric vehicle applications. I also served as an **Attaché Temporaire d'Enseignement et de Recherche (ATER)** at **INSA Toulouse**, contributing to teaching activities in automation, algorithmics, logic, and embedded systems.

My research activities combine model-based and data-driven approaches, with particular interests in:

- Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
- Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI)
- Fault Detection, Isolation and Diagnosis (FDI)
- Prognostics and Predictive Maintenance
- Renewable Energy Systems
- Photovoltaic Power Plants
- Battery Energy Storage Systems
- Smart Grids and Microgrids
- Digital Twins
- Industrial Automation
- Embedded Systems
- Instrumentation and Monitoring Systems
- Data Analytics and Data Mining
- Industry 4.0 and Cyber-Physical Systems

I am currently involved in several national and international research collaborations related to intelligent energy systems, renewable energy integration, digital twins, resilient smart grids, and advanced maintenance strategies. My work seeks to bridge the gap between academic research and industrial deployment by developing practical solutions that can be implemented in real-world environments.

Beyond research, I am actively engaged in teaching and student supervision in the fields of industrial maintenance, automation, artificial intelligence, and energy systems. I strongly believe that the future of engineering relies on the integration of advanced data analytics, intelligent monitoring systems, and sustainable energy technologies to support the next generation of resilient infrastructures.
